I design digital learning experiences that combine pedagogy, interface design, and front-end development — so lessons work smoothly in class, on phones, on tablets, and on a projector.

My focus is micro-lessons: fast, practical activities that build exam skills and everyday communication — without the heavy feel of traditional worksheets.

What I build

This site is a home for interactive lessons, prototypes, and classroom-ready tools. Each activity is designed with clear flow, clean layout, and minimal friction — so students can stay focused on the language task.

  • Responsive layouts for phones, iPads, laptops, and whiteboards
  • Pronunciation, listening, micro-writing, and grammar accuracy tasks
  • Exam-aligned practice (CAE / PTE-style skills)
  • Fast feedback and simple, intuitive interactions

How I design

Learning is an experience. When the interface is calm and predictable, students can use their attention for comprehension, accuracy, and fluency — not figuring out the page.

  • Clear navigation and simple user flows
  • Strong visual rhythm and restrained colour
  • Low cognitive load and readable typography
  • Audio-first support for listening and pronunciation work
  • Accessibility across devices and classroom contexts

Who it’s for

Teachers can use these as warm-ups, targeted skill drills, or extension tasks. Learners can use them independently to strengthen areas that textbooks often miss.
